<p><strong>Bazelgeuse </strong>[beɪːz(ə)lˌdʒəːz]&nbsp;is a&nbsp;<a class="wiki_link" title="" href="/Large+Monsters" target="">Large Monster</a> in <strong>Monster Hunter World (MHW)</strong>.&nbsp;バゼルギウス (爆鱗竜) in Japanese. See <a class="wiki_link" title="here for MHRise Bazel page" href="https://monsterhunterrise.wiki.fextralife.com/Bazelgeuse">here for MHRise Bazel page</a>.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<blockquote> 
 <p>&nbsp;A nefarious&nbsp;flying wyvern that travels the New World in search of prey. It scatters explosive scales over a wide area to prey on whatever gets caught in the blast</p> 
</blockquote> 
<h3 class="bonfire">Bazelgeuse Details &amp; Locations</h3> 
<ul> 
 <li>Found in:&nbsp;All Biomes</li> 
 <li>Target of Quests: <a class="wiki_link" href="/Beyond+the+Blasting+Scales">Beyond the Blasting Scales</a>, <a class="wiki_link" title="" href="/Bazelgeuse+in+the+Field+of+Fire" target="">Bazelgeuse in the Field of Fire</a></li> 
 <li>Is immediately hostile at long range.</li> 
 <li>Attacks other monsters: Yes. Has a cool turf war with <a class="wiki_link" title="Deviljho" href="/Deviljho">Deviljho</a>.</li> 
 <li>Often arrives to your fights with other large monsters and engages in turf wars with them. If you are having trouble tracking or finding it, attack the nearest large monster you can find and it is likely to deal with the hard part of tracking for you.</li> 
 <li>In the Wildspire Waste, when Bazelgeuse&nbsp;charges at you, it can kill Kelbies that in turn will drop their horns.</li> 
 <li>Sizes:&nbsp;<span style="color: #ffcc00;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">♛ </span>1696.97&nbsp;~&nbsp;<span style="color: #ffcc00;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">♛ </span>2410.48</li> 
 <li>Species: Flying Wyverns</li> 
</ul> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<h3 class="bonfire">Bazelgeuse Combat Info</h3> 
<ul> 
 <li>Elemental damages: N/A (Blast)</li> 
 <li>Weak to <img class="lazyload" style="width: nullpx; height: nullpx;" title="mhw-thunder-damage" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/thumbnails/mhws.png" alt="mhw-thunder-damage" width="18" height="18" data-src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/mhw-thunder-damage_s.png"><a class="wiki_link" href="/Thunder+Element">Thunder</a> element, and to <a class="wiki_link" title="" href="/Dragon+Element" target=""><img class="lazyload" style="width: 18px; height: 18px;" title="mhw-dragon-damage" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/thumbnails/mhws.png" alt="mhw-dragon-damage" width="18" height="18" data-src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/mhw-dragon-damage_s.png">Dragon</a> and<img class="lazyload" style="width: 18px; height: 18px;" title="mhw-ice-damage" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/thumbnails/mhws.png" alt="mhw-ice-damage" width="18" height="18" data-src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/mhw-ice-damage_s.png"><a class="wiki_link" title="" href="/Ice+Element" target="">Ice</a> to a lesser degree</li> 
 <li>Can inflict <img title="fireblight-status-effect-mhw_s" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/fireblight-status-effect-mhw_s.png" alt="fireblight-status-effect-mhw_s" width="19" height="19"><a class="wiki_link" title="" href="/Fireblight" target="">Fireblight</a> on hunters.</li> 
 <li>Only moderately susceptible to <a class="wiki_link" title="mhw-poison-status-effect" href="/Poison"><img class="lazyload" style="width: nullpx; height: nullpx;" title="poison" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/thumbnails/mhws.png" alt="mhw-poison-status-effect" width="18" height="18" data-src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/mhw-poison-status-effect-s.png"></a><a class="wiki_link" title="Poison" href="/Poison">Poison</a>, <a class="wiki_link" title="sleep-mhw-status-effect" href="/Sleep"><img class="lazyload" style="width: nullpx; height: nullpx;" title="sleep-mhw-status-effect" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/thumbnails/mhws.png" alt="sleep-mhw-status-effect" width="18" height="18" data-src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/sleep-mhw-status-effect-wiki.png"></a><a class="wiki_link" title="Sleep " href="/Sleep">Sleep</a>&nbsp;and <a class="wiki_link" title="paralysis" href="/Paralysis"><img class="lazyload" style="width: 18px; height: 18px;" title="paralysis" src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/thumbnails/mhws.png" alt="paralysis" width="18" height="18" data-src="/file/Monster-Hunter-World/paralysis-icon.png"></a><a class="wiki_link" title="Paralysis" href="/Paralysis">Paralysis</a></li> 
 <li>The head is the weakest spot, followed by the tail that hunters can cut</li> 
 <li>Hunters can also target the wings, but they are more resistant than the head and tail</li> 
 <li>The chest portion can be broken</li> 
 <li>Friction and heat cause the pods it drops to explode. When it's red, they explode immediately. When it's black, it needs to hit them (including its dive bomb) to make them explode. You can also detonate the pods manually by firing slinger ammos or any projectile to the pods.</li> 
 <li>When in the air, will have 2-3 flying passes to drop pods on the ground, and will dive bomb you on the last run and make them explode</li> 
 <li>Has a rarely-used front fire blast.</li> 
 <li>Likes to dig&nbsp;its head in the ground and charge you, leaving&nbsp;it wide open for attacks when&nbsp;it completes the move.</li> 
 <li>The scales that Bazelgeuse scatters explode with tremendous force, making them extremely deadly. Focus on its legs to knock it down, and then knock out its scale-generating organs under its chin and tail. (Severing the tail reduces the amount of bombs released at once, but the tail stump still keeps a few after the tail is severed.)</li> 
 <li>If Bazelgeuse shows up at an inopportune time, <a class="wiki_link" title="Dung Pods" href="/dung+pod">Dung Pods</a> can be used to scare&nbsp;it away. Carrying dung pods is recommended once Bazelgeuse begins showing up, if you wish to avoid fighting it. However, this is only a temporary solution and the full mechanics regarding Bazelgeuse, dung pods, and possible exploits can be found <a class="wiki_link" href="/Bazelgeuse+Combat+Mechanics">here</a>.</li>

<h3 class="bonfire">Notes &amp; Trivia</h3> 
<ul> 
 <li>Can drop the <a class="wiki_link" title="" href="/Large+Wyvern+Tear" target="">Large Wyvern Tear</a> account item during fights, granting 500RP.</li> 
 <li>Bazelgeuse has been given various nicknames by the community, such as the "B-52 Bomber", and the "Flying *******."</li> 
 <li>Word is, if you say his name three times in a row, he will show up above you and carpet bomb the entire area.</li> 
 <li>Bazelgeuse is known as the "Explosive Scale Wyvern."</li> 
 <li>Bazelgeuse's name is derived from <a class="wiki_link" title="Betelgeuse" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betelgeuse">Betelgeuse</a>&nbsp;(pronounced "beetle-juice"), the brightest star in the Orion constellation. 
  <ul> 
   <li>This&nbsp;star is currently in its last stage of life (red supergiant), and is expected to go supernova (explode) in the near future.</li> 
   <li>"Betelgeuse" also refers to a&nbsp;French nuclear weapons test from 1966.</li> 
  </ul> </li> 
 <li>Runner-Up for the "Most earpiecing Roar in Monster Hunter World" Award</li> 
 <li>BRING. DUNG. PODS.</li> 
</ul>
